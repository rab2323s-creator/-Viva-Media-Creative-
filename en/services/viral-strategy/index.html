Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a viral strategy and a content plan?

A content plan schedules topics. A viral strategy designs repeatable formats, hooks, and distribution that increase retention and sharing — then iterates based on signals. It’s built for reach that compounds.

Can a B2B or service business go viral in GCC or Egypt?

Yes — virality is format-driven, not industry-driven. For B2B and services we use proof-led storytelling, clear outcomes, and series that answer high-intent questions while staying entertaining.

How do you find trends without copying everyone?

We separate the “mechanic” from the “message.” We borrow mechanics (structure, pacing, hook style), and rebuild the angle and story around your positioning so it feels original and brand-safe.

Do you write scripts and hooks, or only strategy?

We can provide hooks, structures, and creator-ready scripting guidelines. If you want full scripting, we connect it with our Copywriting service for tighter conversion messaging.

How do you prevent “viral views” from attracting the wrong audience?

By anchoring content to positioning: clear niche cues, consistent themes, and CTAs aligned to your offer. The goal is growth of the right audience — not empty reach.

Do you manage posting and execution too?

We can. If you want ongoing system execution (calendar, publishing, optimization, brand protection), use Personal Brand Management.
